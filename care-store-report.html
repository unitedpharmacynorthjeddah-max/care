05 — Category Insights

What Sells the Margin: Egypt Beauty & Personal Care

The categories worth building the 60/40 mix around, read against how Egyptian shoppers actually buy — currency-driven brand switching, seasonality, and the local vs. imported split.

  • A

    Face creams & moisturizers

    One of the highest-margin shelves in the store. EGP devaluation pushed many shoppers from imported brands (Nivea, Vaseline, L'Oréal) toward Egyptian-made lines like Eva, Farco, and Macro — same margin band, faster stock turn, and no import/customs exposure. Winter (Nov–Feb) and Ramadan gifting season both spike demand.

  • B

    Sunscreen & SPF

    Egypt's climate makes SPF close to a year-round staple rather than a summer-only item, with the sharpest spike from April through September. Pharmacy-brand sunscreens (La Roche-Posay, Eucerin, ISIS Pharma — Egyptian-made) carry strong margin and shopper trust built through pharmacist recommendation.

  • C

    Hair care & hijab-friendly haircare

    Anti-hair-fall treatments and scalp oils sell strongly year-round in the Egyptian market, often bought on pharmacist recommendation rather than brand alone — a category where staff advice at the counter drives basket size more than shelf placement.

  • D

    Makeup — local brands rising

    Egyptian makeup brands (Sara Beauty, Aya's, local pharmacy-exclusive lines) have gained real shelf share as imported cosmetics got pricier, and they typically carry a wider margin than the international names since there's no distributor markup layered on top.

  • E

    Baby & mother care crossover

    Even within the "staples" bucket, baby lotions and creams sit closer to the beauty margin band than detergents and diapers do — worth tagging separately in the mix rather than lumping with low-margin diapers.

  • F

    Ramadan & Eid seasonality

    Gift-set bundling (perfume + cream + soap) around Ramadan and Eid is a proven basket-size lever in Egyptian pharmacy retail — a natural fit for the white-label line once it launches.
